# Business Email Examples

## Example 1: Firm supplier follow-up

### Input

```text
Use the Business Email Skill.

Recipient: supplier
Goal: ask for shipment update and a firm delivery date
Context: they missed the previous estimated date
Tone: firm but professional
Key points: need tracking number, new delivery date, explanation for delay
Must avoid: sounding angry, accepting extra cost
Language: English
```

### Expected output pattern

```text
Subject: Shipment Update and Revised Delivery Date

Email draft:
Hi [Name],

Could you please send an update on the shipment status? The previous estimated delivery date has passed, so we need the tracking number, the revised delivery date, and a brief explanation for the delay.

Please confirm these details by [date] so we can plan accordingly.

Best,
[Name]

Shorter version:
...

Tone notes:
Firm but does not accept extra cost or make accusations.
```

## Bad output

```text
I am extremely sorry to bother you, but maybe if possible could you tell me what happened?
```

Why bad:

- Too weak.
- No clear ask.
- No deadline.
- Weakens negotiation position.

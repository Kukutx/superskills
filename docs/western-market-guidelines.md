# Western Market Guidelines

Use this document as a general quality filter for outputs aimed at Europe, the UK, North America, Australia, and other Western-facing consumer or business contexts.

## Core positioning rules

Prefer:

- Clear user benefit.
- Plain English.
- Specific claims.
- Trust and privacy signals.
- Natural visual style.
- Inclusive but not forced representation.
- Calm confidence over hype.

Avoid:

- Overpromising.
- Excessive emojis.
- Aggressive discount language unless the brand is discount-led.
- Overly polished AI visuals.
- Fake social proof.
- Fake UI states.
- Overcrowded visual layouts.
- Culturally narrow references unless intentional.

## Copywriting style

Good Western-market copy usually sounds:

- Direct
- Human
- Concrete
- Benefit-led
- Not too formal
- Not too salesy

Avoid common weak phrases:

```txt
revolutionary
ultimate solution
next-generation experience
unlock your potential
seamless and effortless journey
```

Prefer:

```txt
Plan faster
Split costs clearly
Know who is joining
Keep everything in one place
```

## Visual style

Prefer:

- Realistic people and environments.
- Natural imperfections.
- Clear composition.
- Mobile-first readability.
- Strong hierarchy.
- Consistent brand colors.
- Safe margins.

Avoid:

- Plastic-looking skin.
- Distorted hands.
- Overly glossy phone mockups.
- Unreadable tiny UI text.
- Fake street-level addresses.
- AI-generated faces that look synthetic.

## App and SaaS rules

When writing for apps or SaaS:

- Lead with the practical user benefit.
- Do not invent functionality.
- Keep privacy, safety, and control visible when relevant.
- Use short screenshot captions.
- Show realistic UI states.

## Ecommerce rules

When writing for ecommerce:

- Prioritize trust, clarity, delivery expectations, return policy, and product proof.
- Avoid exaggerated claims.
- Prefer concrete product benefits.
- Use plain language for materials, sizing, shipping, and care.

## Creative/IP rules

When a design is inspired by a known franchise, brand, or character:

- Do not copy names, logos, symbols, silhouettes, official colors, or protected character traits.
- Convert the inspiration into original themes, moods, and composition ideas.
- Use original creatures, mascots, badges, icons, and typography.
- Keep the output commercially safer by separating inspiration from execution.
